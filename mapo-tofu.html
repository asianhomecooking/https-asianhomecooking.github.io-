Chinese

Mapo Tofu

⏱ 25 min🍽 Serves 3Heat: 🌶🌶🌶

Ingredients

  • 450 g soft tofu, cubed
  • 150 g minced pork (optional)
  • 2 tbsp doubanjiang (chilli bean paste)
  • 1 tbsp fermented black beans, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorn
  • 250 ml chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • Spring onions to finish

Method

A Sichuan classic: silken tofu in a glossy, numbing-spicy sauce of doubanjiang and Sichuan peppercorns. Comforting, fiery, and dangerously good over plain rice.

  1. Poach the tofu cubes in salted water for 2 minutes — this firms them so they hold together. Drain gently.
  2. Fry the pork until crispy, then add doubanjiang, black beans and garlic. Fry until the oil turns red.
  3. Pour in the stock, slide in the tofu, and simmer for 5 minutes without stirring roughly.
  4. Add the cornstarch slurry and nudge the pan until the sauce turns glossy and clings to the tofu.
  5. Finish with ground Sichuan peppercorn and spring onions. Serve over hot rice.
Cook's tip: Push the tofu with the back of a spoon rather than stirring — it keeps the cubes whole.
