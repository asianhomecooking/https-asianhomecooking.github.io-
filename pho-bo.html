Vietnamese

Phở Bò

⏱ 90 min🍽 Serves 4Heat: 🌶

Ingredients

  • 1.5 L good beef stock
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 5 cm piece of ginger, halved
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 300 g flat rice noodles
  • 200 g beef sirloin, sliced paper-thin
  • Bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime and chilli to serve

Method

Vietnam's iconic beef noodle soup. Real phở takes hours, but this shortcut broth — charred onion and ginger, star anise, cinnamon and good stock — gets you remarkably close on a weekend morning.

  1. Char the onion and ginger directly over a flame or under a hot grill until blackened in spots.
  2. Add them to the stock with star anise and cinnamon. Simmer gently, partly covered, for 45–60 minutes.
  3. Strain the broth, then season with fish sauce and sugar until deeply savoury with a hint of sweetness.
  4. Cook the noodles and divide between bowls. Lay the raw sliced beef on top.
  5. Ladle the boiling broth over the beef — it cooks in seconds. Serve with the herb plate on the side.
Cook's tip: Slice the beef when it is half-frozen — you will get much thinner, more even slices.
